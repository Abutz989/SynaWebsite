Revolutionizing Remote Robotics with Haptic Precision

SYNA is a robotics startup redefining how humans interact with industrial robots.
Instead of building new robotic systems, SYNA develops an intelligent retrofit kit that upgrades existing industrial robots with intuitive haptic control — allowing operators to guide robots with natural precision and touch feedback, as if extending their own hands.
We focus on flexible manufacturing environments in sectors such as defense, medical, aerospace, and hazardous materials, where production demands are complex and constantly changing.
SYNA's technology bridges the gap between human dexterity and robotic endurance, enabling safer, faster, and more adaptable automation without the need for highly trained specialists.

The Growing Gap Between Human Dexterity and Industrial Demand

Advanced manufacturing industries — such as defense and medical sectors — require highly complex production processes involving precise assembly, delicate soldering, and work in cleanrooms or hazardous environments.
At the same time, production volumes are often too low to justify full automation, as each product model is manufactured in limited quantities.
This combination creates a growing dependence on experienced and highly skilled technicians, a workforce that is becoming increasingly scarce.
The shortage of skilled labor leads to significant supply chain bottlenecks and unmet demand, estimated at tens to hundreds of millions of dollars in backlogged orders in the Israeli defense industry alone.
Additionally, remote or hazardous work environments make direct human operation risky and costly, further highlighting the need for flexible robotic solutions that combine safety, precision, and adaptability.

Redefining Human–Robot Collaboration

SYNA’s mission is to make industrial robots as intuitive and responsive as the human hand.
Our proprietary control system uses real-time torque feedback across six degrees of freedom, enabling operators to “feel” the interaction between robot and environment. This allows them to perform complex assembly, inspection, and maintenance tasks remotely and safely, even in sensitive or dangerous settings.
By retrofitting existing robots, SYNA lowers automation costs, shortens deployment time, and gives manufacturers the flexibility to respond quickly to changing production needs.
The result is a new standard of human-robot collaboration that unlocks efficiency, precision, and safety — while directly addressing the skilled-labor shortage in advanced manufacturing.
